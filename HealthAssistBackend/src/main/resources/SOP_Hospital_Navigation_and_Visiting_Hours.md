# Hospital Navigation & Visiting Hours SOP
[KB-ID: KB-001]

## Purpose
This SOP explains how patients and visitors can navigate the hospital safely and efficiently, including entrances, parking, wayfinding, and visiting policies. The goal is to reduce confusion, improve flow at peak hours, and ensure safety in critical areas such as ICU and Emergency. All responses referencing this SOP should be **informational only** and must avoid clinical advice.

## Entrances & Building Access
The **Main Entrance** operates from **06:00–22:00** daily to balance accessibility with nighttime security. After hours, all pedestrian traffic is funneled through the **Emergency Entrance**, which is staffed around the clock. Security personnel are trained to verify visitor intent, check IDs if necessary, and direct individuals to the appropriate care areas. This policy helps the hospital maintain secure access while prioritizing urgent care and staff operations during nighttime hours.

## Parking & Drop-Off
Parking is available in **P1** and **P2**, with the first **30 minutes free** to support quick drop-offs, document runs, and pickup of medications. After that, rates apply and can be settled at automated pay stations accepting **cash, UPI, and major cards**. Designated **accessible parking** bays are closest to entrances to accommodate patients and visitors with limited mobility. We encourage caregivers to use the **drop-off lane** for patients with mobility support needs and then proceed to park to avoid bottlenecks.

## Visiting Hours & Guidelines
Visiting hours are designed to balance family presence with patient rest and care team workflows:
- **General Wards**: **10:00–12:00** and **17:00–19:00**. Up to two visitors per patient help maintain a calm environment.
- **ICU/CCU**: **11:00–11:30** and **18:00–18:30** with **one visitor** subject to **nurse approval**. Due to the critical nature of care, visits are shorter and controlled.
- **Pediatrics**: One parent/caregiver is permitted **24/7**, supporting continuity of care and comfort. A second visitor is allowed during general hours.
- **Maternity**: A partner may remain **24/7**; up to two additional visitors may enter during general visiting hours.
- **Isolation Rooms**: Visitors must follow posted **PPE** requirements and seek guidance from the nurse station to prevent cross-infection.

## Wayfinding & Locations
To minimize confusion, each tower and level is clearly labeled:
- **Reception**: Ground Floor, Main Lobby — primary point for directions and assistance.
- **Radiology**: Tower B, Level 1 — imaging services including X-Ray, CT, MRI, and Ultrasound.
- **Cardiology OPD**: Tower A, Level 2 — outpatient consults, testing coordination.
- **Pharmacy**: Main Lobby (Front) for general prescriptions; **Discharge Pharmacy** at Tower A, Level 1 for post-discharge medications.
- **Billing & Insurance**: Tower A, Level 0 — payments, insurance queries, and coverage verification.
- **Sample Collection**: Tower B, Level 0 — blood draws and routine specimen collection.

## Accessibility & Support
Wheelchairs are available at the Main Entrance with a refundable deposit to ensure availability and care. Elevators in both towers serve all patient-facing levels and are prioritized for patients, elderly visitors, and those with mobility needs. For real-time assistance, visitors may dial **“0”** from any lobby courtesy phone to reach the front desk for wayfinding and accessibility support.

## Etiquette & Safety
To protect privacy and infection control, photography is discouraged unless **express consent** is obtained. Signage may indicate **masking** requirements during respiratory illness seasons. Alcohol-based hand sanitizers are available at entrances and ward doors; visitors are encouraged to sanitize hands before and after each visit. Flowers and perishable items are **not permitted** in ICU areas due to infection control concerns.

## Edge Cases & Escalations
When a family has special circumstances (e.g., end-of-life visitation, caregiver dependency), the assistant should instruct them to speak with the **Nurse-in-Charge** or the **Duty Administrator**. Security incidents or lost-and-found queries should be routed to hospital security (internal **333**). In all cases, staff should remain empathetic and provide clear next steps rather than rigid denials.

## FAQs (Grounded Answers)
**Q:** What are ICU visiting hours?  
**A:** ICU/CCU visits run **11:00–11:30** and **18:00–18:30**, limited to **one visitor** with **nurse approval**. *[KB-001 §Visiting Hours & Guidelines]*

**Q:** Where do I pay my bill and clarify insurance?  
**A:** **Billing & Insurance** are at **Tower A, Level 0**. *[KB-001 §Wayfinding & Locations]*
